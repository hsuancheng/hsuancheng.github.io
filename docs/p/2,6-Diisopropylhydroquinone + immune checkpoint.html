Targeting the epigenome has been considered a compelling treatment modality for several cancers, including gliomas. Nearly 80% of the lower-grade gliomas and secondary glioblastomas harbor recurrent mutations in isocitrate dehydrogenase (). Mutant IDH generates high levels of -hydroxyglutarate (-HG) that inhibit various components of the epigenetic machinery, including histone and DNA demethylases.
